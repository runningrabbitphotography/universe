WHY THINGS FALL

  • Gravity is the force that pulls on things and makes them fall.
  • If you throw a ball into the air, gravity makes it come back down.
  • How much you weigh is based on gravity. You weigh more on Earth than you do on the Moon, which has less gravity.
  • In space there is no gravity, so everything floats.
  • High and low tides on Earth are caused by the Moon's gravity.
  • Gravity keeps the Earth and other planets in orbit around the Sun.

HOW WE CAN SEE

  • Light is a type of energy that allows us to see things around us like a lamp or the Sun and the Moon.
  • Light takes time to reach our eyes. Light left the Sun 8 minutes ago. Light left the moon 1 second ago.
  • That means that the light we see is older than when the light was sent.
  • Light from a Star twinkles as it creates energy, but light from a Planet reflects the light of a star and does not twinkle.
  • Light from the Sun gives us warmth and life. Plants need sunlight to live.
  • Light is the fastest thing we know of.

HOW LONG IT TAKES TO GET THERE

  • Distance is how far away something is from something else.
  • A trip from Earth to the nearest Star (Proxima Centauri), with today's rockets, takes about 73 thousand years.
  • A trip from Earth to Mars, with today's rockets, takes about 7 months.
  • A trip from Earth to the Moon, with today's rockets, takes about 3 days.
