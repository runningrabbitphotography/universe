WHY DO THINGS FALL?

  1. Gravity is the force that pulls on things and makes them fall.

  2. If you throw a ball into the air, gravity makes it come back down.

  3. How much you weigh involves gravity. You weigh more on Earth than you do on the Moon, which has less gravity.

  4. In space there is no gravity, so everything floats.

  5. High and low tides on Earth are caused by the Moon's gravity.

  6. Gravity keeps the Earth and other Planets in orbit around the Sun.

  7. Why Do Apples Fall From A Tree?

WHAT IS LIGHT?

  1. Light is a type of energy that allows us to see things around us. The Sun produces its own light, while the Moon reflects light.

  2. Light takes time to reach our eyes. The light we see left the Sun about 8 minutes ago. The light we see left the moon a little over 1 second ago.

  3. That means that the light we see is older than when the light was sent.

  4. Light from our nearest Star (Proxima Centauri), takes a little over 4 years to reach Earth. Which means we are seeing that Star as it was over 4 years ago.

  5. Light from a Star twinkles as it creates its own energy, but light from a Planet reflects the light of a star and does not twinkle.

  6. Light from the Sun gives us warmth and life. Plants need sunlight to live.

  7. Light is the fastest thing we know of. It travels about 186,000 miles per second.

  8. Why Does Sunlight Disappear At Night?

HOW LONG DOES IT TAKE TO GET THERE?

  1. Distance is how far away something is from something else.

  2. A trip from Earth to the nearest Star (Proxima Centauri), with today's rockets, takes about 73 thousand years.

  3. A trip from Earth to Mars, with today's rockets, takes about 7 months.

  4. A trip from Earth to the Moon, with today's rockets, takes about 3 days.
